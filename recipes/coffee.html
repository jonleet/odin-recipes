Coffee

Recipe Description

This recipe is a how to make pourover coffee using a Chemex. This is a different method from using a regular drip maker, espresso, V60, or french press.

Ingredients and Equipment

  • Recently roasted coffee beans
  • Water
  • Chemex filter
  • Chemex coffee maker
  • Scale
  • Gooseneck kettle
  • Burr grinder

Steps

  1. Heat water in the gooseneck kettle 205Â°F.
  2. In the meantime, grind 20 grams of coffee beans.
  3. Once water reaches appropriate temperature, place filter in Chemex, and wet the filter.
  4. Dump water out of Chemex that was used to wet filter.
  5. Pour coffee ground into filter.
  6. Place Chemex on scale and tare the scale.
  7. Pour 320 grams of water onto the beans in circular motions. Once the beans bloom, let the water make its way through the grounds before adding more water. Continue until all of the 320 grams of water has been added.
  8. Pour excess hot water into mug in order to warm the mug.
  9. Once coffee has been completely extracted, dispose of filter and coffee grounds
  10. Enjoy!
